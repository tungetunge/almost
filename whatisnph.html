What is Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus (NPH)?

Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus (NPH) is a brain condition that mainly affects older adults. It's often misdiagnosed, but when recognized early, it can be successfully treated.

Key Fact: Early diagnosis and intervention can significantly improve quality of life.

The Three Main Symptoms

1. Walking Problems

  • Difficulty starting to walk
  • Shuffling or magnetic gait
  • Feeling unsteady or off-balance
  • Frequent falls or fear of falling
  • Wide-based stance when standing

2. Bladder Problems

  • Sudden urgent need to urinate
  • Frequent urination
  • Loss of bladder control (incontinence)
  • Getting up at night to urinate

Note: Bladder problems are often the first symptom to appear.

3. Memory & Thinking Changes

  • Mild memory problems
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Slower thinking or responses
  • Mood changes or apathy
  • Less interest in activities

Causes

Idiopathic NPH (iNPH)

  • No clear cause, common in adults over 60
  • Possibly linked to aging or CSF flow issues

Secondary NPH

  • Caused by head injury, surgery, or infection
  • Linked to brain bleeding (e.g., subarachnoid hemorrhage)

In both cases, CSF buildup enlarges ventricles, compressing brain tissue and causing symptoms.

Clinical Assessment

Brain Imaging

  • CT/MRI to detect ventriculomegaly
  • Uses Evans Index for measurement

Lumbar Puncture

  • Tap test to assess gait improvement

CSF Drainage Tests

  • Extended drainage to predict shunt response

Accurate diagnosis is vital to distinguish NPH from other neurodegenerative disorders.

Treatment

Shunt Surgery

  • Drains excess CSF to the abdomen
  • Improves gait in many patients

Monitoring

  • Regular check-ups for shunt function

Supportive Care

  • Physical therapy and cognitive rehab
  • Bladder management support

Early intervention, especially in idiopathic NPH, offers the best outcomes.
